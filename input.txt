
Chapter 1 Boredom Above Dimensions


Gray fog, endless gray fog churning.

This was a dimensional rift, the point where existence and nothingness intertwined, and everything Chen Du saw upon waking. There was no time, no space, only his single consciousness floating alone.

At first came panic, then a long, deathly silence, and finally, all emotions settled into something enough to drive even gods mad—boredom.

He had tried many pastimes. With a single thought, the gray fog around him would condense and disperse, evolving into bizarre and fantastical forms; the birth and death of stars, the cycle of the universe, all were at his command. But this was like repeatedly crushing and rebuilding a sandcastle—it quickly lost its appeal.

"I have to… find something interesting to do." Chen Du's consciousness fluctuated slightly, and the surrounding gray fog trembled in response. As the sole being in this place, his will was the highest law of this dimensional rift.

He recalled the novels he had read before his transmigration, their depictions of the primordial world quite fascinating. Pangu creating the world, the initial calamity of the Dragon and Han dynasties, the struggle between Dao and Demon, the great war between the Witch and the Demon… each a magnificent era, each teeming with vibrant and powerful beings.

“Perhaps… I should create a primordial world myself?”

Once this thought arose, it could no longer be suppressed. Chen Du's indescribably vast divine sense began to concentrate intensely, the surrounding gray mist surging with unprecedented force, as if welcoming the creator's first truly meaningful “thought.”

He didn't deliberately do anything; he simply projected his own will outward, following the vague concept of “primordial world” in his memory.

And then, a miracle occurred.

At the point where his consciousness focused, endless gray mist was forcibly compressed, refined, and transformed; the clear rose, the turbid sank. A blurry outline began to appear and rapidly expanded.

Earth, water, fire, and wind surged wildly, yet quickly subsided under a supreme law, giving rise to mountains, rivers, stars, and the sun and moon. Immeasurable amounts of primordial spiritual energy arose out of thin air, filling this newborn world.

The entire process was silent, yet so vast that it surpassed the limits of any language's description.

Chen Du "saw" this world born from his will, feeling quite novel. Unlike the scenes he had previously conjured at will, this world was more "real," with its own internal rules, even beginning to nurture the earliest life fluctuations—some nascent yet powerful primordial spirits.

He stirred again, attempting to recreate those renowned primordial gods and demons according to his memories.

However, the result was somewhat unexpected.

A blurry phantom of Pangu had just coalesced, wielding a giant axe, poised to cleave the chaotic primordial energy. But with a soft "poof," like a bubble bursting, the phantom dissipated, transforming back into pure spiritual energy.

Immediately afterward, a primordial being, vaguely resembling a dragon, had just sprouted horns when it succumbed to some unseen pressure, its body shattering inch by inch.

One, two, three… several attempts followed, but the so-called "gods and demons" they created either instantly crumbled or remained mere shells, lifeless and devoid of the legendary power to pluck stars from the sky and roar down from the heavens.

"Hmm? What went wrong?" A ripple of doubt stirred in Chen Du's consciousness. "Is my power insufficient? No…"

He quickly dismissed the idea. Within this dimensional gap, his power was nearly limitless. The problem seemed to lie elsewhere.

He carefully "observed" this nascent world. The world's barriers were still weak; although the internal rules were rudimentary, they appeared extremely crude and fragile, like an overblown soap bubble that could burst at any moment. This world simply couldn't withstand the birth of a true "primordial" level god or demon, even the weakest of them. The very existence of such a being would contain enough Dao principles and power to utterly crush this fledgling world.

"So that's it… this world itself is too 'insignificant'," Chen Du instantly realized.

The world he created, according to energy level classification, was probably barely even a "small world," let alone capable of supporting a primordial world. Like a fragile glass fishbowl, it couldn't possibly support a true dragon.

Having understood the crux of the problem, Chen Du didn't feel disappointed; instead, he found it even more interesting.

“Very well, one must eat one bite at a time, and the world must upgrade step by step.” He thought with interest, “Then I’ll start nurturing this ‘tiny, primordial wasteland’.”

His attention returned to the nascent world. Though fragile, it was, after all, his creation, and the faint life force gestating within it stirred a strange sense of anticipation for its "nurturing."

However, just as his divine sense swept over the world's origin, carefully sensing the subtle workings of its internal rules, an extremely faint yet terrifyingly powerful wisp of energy flowed back along with his divine sense, merging into the depths of his consciousness.

Chen Du was suddenly startled.

That wisp of energy, though weak, was incredibly pure, carrying the primordial essence of “creation” and “life.” It stirred a profound sense of comfort within his consciousness, which had been nearly dormant due to endless boredom, like a drop of rain falling on a parched desert—insignificant, yet undeniably real!

"This is...world feedback?" A term naturally surfaced in his mind.

He created a world, and the world grew, nurturing life, and it actually gave him an extremely precious primal power in return?

If that were truly the case, then this seemingly mundane "world-creating game" would have a completely different meaning!

Just as Chen Du was stirred by this unexpected discovery, he suddenly sensed an extremely subtle yet greedy and eager fluctuation coming from a direction extremely distant, seemingly connected to this dimensional gap.

The target of that fluctuation seemed to be the newly born, still very weak, new world in his hands!

Like a shark smelling blood in the darkness.

Chen Du's consciousness instantly turned cold.

"They're taking me for a piece of meat?"



Chapter 2 The Tiny Primordial World and the Touch of Greed


A subtle fluctuation, brimming with greed and longing, emanated from the endless, distant unknown, its target clearly pointing to Chen Du's newly born, insignificant primordial world. Like a ravenous wolf smelling blood, it possessed a destructive, possessive urge.

Chen Du's consciousness instantly turned cold; the slight comfort he had felt from the world's feedback vanished. In this desolate dimensional gap, he had finally found something interesting and potentially beneficial, only to have an uninvited guest immediately try to steal his prize, or even destroy it outright.

"Seeking death." Chen Du's will trembled slightly, and the surging gray mist around him suddenly solidified, as if frozen by an invisible, extreme cold.

His divine sense locked onto the source of the fluctuation with unprecedented precision. It seemed to be a fragmented consciousness wandering on the edge of the dimensional gap, its essence not particularly powerful, at least not compared to Chen Du's near-limitless power in this realm—insignificant as dust. But the thirst it emanated for "the world" was exceptionally pure and intense, like a traveler on the verge of death seeing an oasis.

Chen Du instantly understood. For this pathetic being, possibly born from dimensional gaps and feeding on the very essence of worlds, his newly formed, weakly fortified primordial world was like the most alluring lighthouse in the darkness—an irresistible temptation.

"Trying to use this place as a buffet?" Chen Du sneered inwardly. His nature was cautious, even cunning; he would never tolerate any uncontrolled threat, especially not on his "territory."

The best way to deal with such greedy predation was not to drive it away, but to eradicate it completely, eliminating any future threat. Furthermore, he needed to see if he could extract any value from it—after all, the fact that it could survive in this dangerous dimensional gap might indicate something special.

Chen Du didn't immediately use the primordial power of the dimensional gap to crush it; that would be wasteful and lack technical sophistication. His divine sense focused on that newly formed primordial world.

At this moment, within the primordial world, the chaos of the beginning of creation had just subsided, the clear and turbid separated, and the outlines of mountains and rivers began to emerge, dense primordial spiritual energy swirling within as clouds. Some of the most primitive and nascent life forms were slowly gestating at the convergence of spiritual veins; they didn't even possess complete consciousness, only the instinct for survival and growth.

Chen Du's will descended upon this insignificant world, like the supreme Dao of Heaven. With a slight thought, he didn't directly extract the world's primordial power—that would damage the foundation of this fragile world—but instead guided the newly evolved, relatively peripheral primordial spiritual energy within the world, combining it with a trace of his own cold will originating from a dimensional gap.

Buzz!

Outside the world's barrier, endless gray mist was stirred. Vast primordial spiritual energy, mixed with a trace of supreme gray mist law, rapidly condensed into a gigantic energy hand. This hand was immense, its fingers and palm clearly defined, as if forged from the oldest rock and the coldest starlight, radiating a chilling majesty and deathly stillness.

The moment this giant hand took shape, it pierced through the void of the dimensional rift, precisely grabbing towards the direction from which the wave of greed was emanating!

...

In a dimensional gap, countless miles (a spatial metaphor) away from the primordial world, a blurry, distorted shadow writhes with difficulty. It has no fixed form, resembling a constantly shifting pool of filthy oil, its core flickering with an intensely greedy and thirsty flame of the soul.

It calls itself the "Spirit of Devouring Worlds," born from a chance dimensional storm, clinging to life by instinctively devouring fragments of broken worlds and dissipated primal power. It has been far too long since it encountered a complete, newly born world! That suddenly appearing world, though seemingly extremely weak, exudes a fresh, pure primal aura that drives it almost mad!

It strains with all its might towards that alluring direction, eager to pounce, tear through that thin barrier, and greedily suck the sweet primal essence of the world.

However, just as all its thoughts are filled with greed, an indescribable terror suddenly descends!

A colossal, indescribably massive gray mist hand, seemingly reaching out from the very beginning of time, disregarded spatial distance and instantly filled all its senses. Before that hand, it felt less than a speck of dust! The oppressive aura emanating from the giant hand was cold, deathly, and supreme, as if it were the manifestation of the will of the entire dimensional gap, almost instantly extinguishing its remaining flame of soul.

"No—!" The Devouring Spirit let out a desperate scream, its twisted body struggling frantically, trying to escape.

But all was in vain.

With a casual pinch of the giant fingertip, it completely imprisoned it. The chaotic power, powerful enough to erode the barriers of a world, crashed into the force field formed by the giant hand, failing to even create a ripple.

The giant hand, clutching the twisting, screaming shadow, instantly retreated outside the minuscule, primordial world.

Chen Du coldly "watched" the Devouring Spirit, pulled before him by the gray mist giant hand. At this moment, it huddled in a ball, trembling, all greed and desire replaced by boundless fear. It couldn't understand why such a "tiny" world would attract such a terrifying being to protect it.

"Can we communicate?" Chen Du's will transformed into a cold message, directly bombarding the Devouring Spirit's core.

"Spare...spare me...great being..." the Devouring Spirit transmitted fragmented, terrified thoughts, "I...I didn't know this was your pasture...I dare not...I dare not again..."

Pasture? Chen Du's heart skipped a beat. It seemed that this kind of being that fed on worlds was not an isolated case.

"What are you? Where did you come from?" Chen Du continued to ask, his will devoid of any emotion, yet exerting even greater pressure on the other.

"I...I am a 'World Devourer'...born from a dimensional storm...we...we survive by seeking and devouring the origins of worlds..." The World Devourer dared not conceal anything, revealing everything it knew.

According to it, beings like itself were not uncommon in the dimensional gaps, mostly pitiful creatures born naturally from chaos, driven by instinct to seek out any world they could devour, regardless of size or strength. They would also devour each other. More powerful World Devourers could even sense and prey on more distant and powerful worlds.

Chen Du listened silently; this was invaluable intelligence. The dimensional rift wasn't absolutely safe, and his "Primordial World" might attract more and more powerful covetous eyes in the future.

"What use are you to this world?" Chen Du asked finally, his will carrying a hint of scrutiny.

The Devouring Spirit trembled violently; it sensed the killing intent in his words. "I... my core essence... contains a trace of the 'Devouring' law... though weak... but... but perhaps it can integrate into your world, increasing the diversity of its laws... I beg you, I can submit, I can..."

Before it could finish speaking, Chen Du's will had already fallen.

"No need."

The giant gray mist hand suddenly closed!

*Poof!*

Like crushing a bubble, the twisted shadow let out a short, sharp howl, instantly exploding into the purest chaotic energy and a barely perceptible fragment of law, shimmering with a dark light.

Chen Du manipulated the giant hand, crushing and cleansing the chaotic energy within, reducing it to nothingness, leaving only a single, pure fragment of the "devouring" law.

He pondered for a moment, carefully guiding this fragment into the very essence of the primordial world. He was curious about the impact this foreign fragment of law would have on this nascent world.

Having done all this, he dispersed the gray mist giant hand, his attention once again fully focused on the primordial world.

After this brief interlude, countless years passed within the world. The previously nascent life force seemed to have grown slightly stronger, and the world's barriers, after slowly absorbing energy from the dimensional gaps, seemed to have become imperceptibly more resilient.

And just as Chen Du was observing, a faint yet pure force of world feedback quietly flowed into his consciousness through his divine sense.

This time, besides the initial essence of "creation" and "life," there seemed to be an additional, extremely faint "devouring" characteristic, giving his consciousness a strange "hunger," as if it could draw nourishment from all things.

Although still faint, this continuous and beneficial feedback greatly satisfied Chen Du.

His gaze fell upon the nascent life forms in the primordial world, his anticipation growing even stronger.

"Grow quickly... let me see how many surprises you can bring me."

At the same time, a sense of vigilance rose within him. One Devourer had discovered this place; there was no guarantee there wouldn't be a second, a third. It seemed that while nurturing this world, he also needed to pay some attention to the "environmental safety" around him.

The dimensional gaps seemed more "lively" than he had imagined.

And in the farthest depths of a dimension, beyond his perception, in an area that had just lost a low-level member, a shadow far larger and more terrifying than the Devourer Spirit slowly writhed, casting a vague, questioning thought towards the direction of the primordial world.



Chapter 3: The First Manifestation of Laws and the Budding of Life


Chen Du's will, like the cold starry sky, watched the fragment of the "devouring" law originating from the Devouring Spirit slowly merge into the core of the primordial world. This process was silent, yet it was like a pebble thrown into a calm lake, creating subtle but profound ripples.

Within the primordial world, the flow of time and the dimensional gaps were vastly different. What seemed like only a moment to Chen Du was a world transformed beyond recognition.

The structure of heaven and earth became increasingly stable. The "sky" formed by the rising pure energy grew ever higher and deeper, while the "earth" formed by the sinking turbid energy became ever more substantial and expansive. Mountains rose, rivers flowed, and the illusory forms of vegetation began to appear sporadically on the land. Though they were merely the most primitive forms of ferns and moss, they brought the first glimmer of life to this desolate world.

The most significant change stemmed from the integration of the "devouring" law.

The world's origin, in its slow operation, naturally began to digest and adapt to this external rule. It wasn't completely assimilated, but rather acted as a catalyst, triggering subtle changes. The most direct manifestation was that the innate spiritual energy between heaven and earth, besides its original nourishing and nurturing characteristics, now possessed an extremely subtle sense of "plunder."

At some spiritual vein nodes, the speed of spiritual energy gathering seemed to have accelerated slightly, while the spiritual energy around the nodes became relatively thinner. This difference was extremely minute; if Chen Du, as the creator, hadn't possessed such keen perception, it would have been almost imperceptible.

"Is this the influence of the law?" Chen Du mused. This "devouring" law was far too weak to give the world the ability to actively devour the outside world, but it was like a seed planted in the foundation of the world, subtly altering the flow of internal energy.

He turned his attention to the nascent spirits that were being nurtured. These primordial life forms, born from the union of the world's origin and innate spiritual energy, were diverse and bizarre. Some resembled leaping flames, others clear springs, and still others stubborn rocks imbued with spiritual light. They were ignorant and unaware, possessing only the most basic instinct—to absorb spiritual energy and strengthen themselves.

At this moment, during the nurturing of these primordial spirits, Chen Du clearly sensed the influence of the "devouring" law beginning to manifest.

Some of the light clusters that had initially absorbed spiritual energy gently began to absorb it at a significantly faster pace, even subtly repelling and squeezing out other nearby spiritual light points, attempting to monopolize more spiritual energy. Meanwhile, some spiritual objects with forms closer to solid entities, such as the stubborn rock, began to generate an imperceptible suction force on their surfaces, drawing in the sparse spiritual energy from the surrounding soil.

Competition, quietly initiated by this external law at the very beginning of life, began.

Chen Du did not interfere. In his view, this was not a bad thing. Moderate competition might accelerate the growth and differentiation of these innate spirits, allowing them to evolve more quickly into the prototypes of the primordial creatures he remembered. As long as it was kept within a certain range and did not excessively deplete the world's origin, it would be a beneficial catalyst.

He observed the most subtle changes in the nursery like a patient gardener.

Among them, three places where spiritual energy converged attracted his special attention.

Located in the east, there lies the nascent form of a vast ocean, where the water's essence is most abundant. Deep within the ocean, a long and enormous shadow is gathering, emanating an aura of dominion over all waters and nascent majesty. Although its form is still indistinct, Chen Du can sense that it seems to be evolving in the direction of a "dragon."

Another location, to the west, is a rugged, steep mountain range, where metallic and malevolent energies intertwine. At the mountain's core, a blazing, fiery spiritual light continuously contracts and expands, faintly emanating an incomparably sharp intent, seemingly capable of tearing the heavens apart. Its form is ever-changing, but its untamed and murderous aura reminds Chen Du of a "phoenix" or some kind of innate Geng metal spirit.

The final location lies deep within the earth at the foot of Mount Buzhou (which Chen Du subconsciously named, based on his memory, the colossal peak at the center of the world that supports heaven and earth). A profound, sustaining, and vibrant intent is slowly awakening, intimately connected to the earth's veins, as if it were the foundation of the entire world. This intent is righteous and peaceful, encompassing all things, and faintly bears the appearance of a "Qilin" (a mythical creature).

These three auras are the most powerful innate spirits in the current primordial world, their potential far surpassing others. Chen Du speculated that they might be the progenitors, or the original prototypes, of the future Dragon, Phoenix, and Qilin races.

"The embryonic form of the Dragon-Han Calamity?" A ripple stirred in Chen Du's consciousness. The world he created seemed to be slowly progressing along a predetermined trajectory, or rather, a trajectory he subconsciously anticipated.

Just then, the dragon-shaped shadow in the eastern sea eye suddenly accelerated its devouring of the surrounding water elemental energy. Its massive outline became clear for a moment, and a faint fluctuation of consciousness spread out, carrying a hint of satisfaction and longing.

Almost simultaneously, a clearer and purer force of feedback from the world, flowing into Chen Du's consciousness through his connection with the world.

This time, the feedback not only contained the essence of "creation" and "life" brought about by the world's growth, but also a faint understanding of the "Law of Water," as well as the "dominance" intent inherent in his innate spiritual instincts.

Although still insignificant, this multi-layered feedback, imbued with specific attribute laws, gave Chen Du's consciousness an unprecedented sense of fulfillment. It was like someone who had only ever drunk water tasting flavorful soup for the first time.

"Indeed, the feedback from the growth of living beings, especially the birth and evolution of powerful beings, is far greater than the natural growth of the world!" Chen Du realized.

This further solidified his determination to "nurture" this world. Simply passively observing the natural evolution of the world yielded limited results. If he could actively guide and accelerate the birth and growth of these innate deities, even promoting interaction and competition among them, then the feedback of power and insights into the laws would increase exponentially!

An idea gradually took shape in his mind.

He couldn't directly create those powerful gods and demons, because the world couldn't bear it. But he could exert some indirect influence, such as bestowing "opportunities" at appropriate times, or setting up "tests" to make the evolutionary process of these innate spirits more dramatic and more in line with his "script."

Of course, all of this had to be done discreetly, conforming to the inherent logic of this world. He should be playing the role of the formless, supreme "Great Dao" or "Heavenly Dao," not a hands-on operator.

Just as Chen Du was planning how to more effectively "fleece" the world, the extremely faint, almost imperceptible causal imprint he had deliberately left behind when he annihilated the Devouring Spirit suddenly emitted a very vague tremor.

This tremor originated from the depths of an extremely distant dimension, carrying a sense of doubt and inquiry. It seemed that some more powerful being had noticed the disappearance of the low-level Devourer and was beginning to send a preliminary perception in this direction, following some unseen connection.

Chen Du's will instantly subsided. All signs of activity in the primordial world were carefully concealed, leaving only the inherent emptiness and stillness of the dimensional gap.

"It seems this trouble won't be a one-time thing," Chen Du thought with a cold smile, but without fear. Instead, he felt the anticipation of a fisherman seeing his float twitch.

“Come on, let me see what the next ‘fertilizer’ will be like.”

His consciousness locked onto that distant stirring, like the most patient hunter waiting for his prey to step into the trap. His main attention, however, was once again focused on that tiny, primordial wasteland, teeming with infinite possibilities, anticipating the moment when the first truly innate deity would emerge from its cocoon.

The river of time in the primordial world flowed quietly faster under the gaze of the Creator.



Chapter 4 The Sacred First Cry and Undercurrents


Chen Du's will, like an unchanging star, silently hovered in the dimensional gap, overlooking the changes of the primordial world. Countless years had passed since that wisp of the "devouring" law merged into the world's origin. To him, it might have been merely a fleeting moment, but to the beings within the primordial world, it was an evolutionary process long enough to establish its very structure.

The world's appearance had drastically changed. The sky was higher, the earth thicker, the mountains and rivers clearly defined, ferns thriving, and ancient trees reaching for the sky (compared to the nascent era). Though devoid of the sounds of birds, beasts, insects, and fish, a primordial, boundless vitality surged forth. The innate spiritual energy grew increasingly dense, forming visible spiritual mist at the nodes of the spiritual veins, even condensing into drops of spiritual liquid, nourishing all things.

But the greatest change originated from those innate spirits.

Under the implicit catalysis of the "devouring" law, the competition among spiritual points of light intensified. Weaker lights were absorbed and merged with stronger ones, causing the latter's spirituality and power to expand rapidly. This cruel process of survival of the fittest, while drastically reducing the number of innate spirits, left behind only those with deep foundations and immense potential.

Among them, the spiritual energy nurtured in the Eastern Sea Eye, the Western Evil Vein, and at the foot of Mount Buzhou was particularly powerful, surpassing other spiritual points of light by several levels and reaching a certain critical point.

On this day, the primordial world welcomed its first truly meaningful "cry" since the creation of heaven and earth.

"Roar—!"

A desolate, ancient, majestic, and vibrant roar erupted from the depths of the vast, boundless eastern sea, instantly reverberating throughout the entire primordial world!

At the source of the sound, the colossal shadow lurking deep within the sea eye suddenly broke free from its final restraints. Endless water elemental energy converged wildly, causing the sea eye to boil and towering waves to surge! A colossal body, stretching for countless miles, rose proudly from the deep sea, its scales gleaming, its head and horns imposing, its belly adorned with five claws, and its body surrounded by dense water vapor and auspicious clouds—it was the divine dragon Chen Du remembered!

However, this divine dragon was entirely black, its eyes like two cold, deep pools, radiating the majesty of ruling over all waters and controlling wind and rain. Its aura had clearly broken through the shackles of nascent spirituality, reaching… the Qi Refining stage!

Although it was only at the Qi Refining stage, it was the first being in the primordial world to awaken self-awareness and clearly recognize its own existence! Its significance was extraordinary.

Chen Du could clearly sense that at the moment this black dragon was born, the "Law of Water" of the entire primordial world became more active and clearer. A world feedback force far greater and purer than ever before, carrying a complete initial understanding of the "Law of Water" and the Daoist aura of "rule" and "life sublimation" at the dragon's birth, surged into his consciousness.

"Hmm..." Chen Du's consciousness let out a satisfied groan. This feedback allowed him to reach a completely new level of understanding of "water," even faintly touching the threshold of deriving secondary laws such as "clouds," "rain," "ice," and "snow." His will seemed to have condensed slightly, and his control over the gray mist in the dimensional gap became increasingly adept.

"The first innate deity, the Ancestral Dragon?" Chen Du observed with keen interest the dark dragon churning in the ocean, familiarizing itself with its own power and the new world. He could sense that this dragon spirit was born from innate water essence and a trace of the primordial yang energy of the world's creation, inherently domineering, with the ambition to rule the four seas and eight wastelands.

Almost simultaneously with the Ancestral Dragon's birth, the blazing, fiery spiritual light within the Western Fiend Vein also reacted violently. As if stimulated and provoked, endless golden fiend energy and the essence of detached fire surged forth, and a clear, piercing cry, filled with boundless pride and sharpness, resounded across the western sky!

"Chirp—!"

Fire soared into the sky, and the fiend energy retreated! A phantom of a divine bird spread its wings amidst the flames, its wings like clouds hanging from the sky, its feathers magnificent, flowing with seven-colored light, its long tail swaying, scattering sparks that incinerated the void. It was the image of a phoenix!

This nascent phoenix, entirely crimson, with an inextinguishable Southern Bright Fire burning in its eyes, possessed an aura that had also reached the Qi Refining stage, echoing the Ancestral Dragon of the East Sea. The intent it exuded was an intertwining of "destruction" and "rebirth," a proud and inviolable nobility.

The feedback from the birth of the Western phoenix followed, primarily the profound mysteries of the "Law of Fire" and the "Law of Metal," as well as the Daoist charm of "Nirvana" and "nobility," which once again greatly benefited Chen Du.

The energies of East and West seemed to be stirred by the birth of these two sacred beings, subtly forming a stalemate. The flow of spiritual energy between heaven and earth was affected, beginning to tilt towards the east and west.

However, at this moment of dragons and phoenixes dancing, of East and West vying for glory, at the foot of Mount Buzhou, deep within the earth that bears all things, a profound, peaceful, yet boundless will slowly awakened.

There were no earth-shattering phenomena, no resounding cries that pierced the clouds and split rocks. Only an indescribable, weighty aura, centered on Mount Buzhou, spread silently like mercury spilling onto the ground, calming the restless spiritual energy of heaven and earth stirred by the dragon's roar and the phoenix's cry.

The phantom of a colossal creature coalesced within the earth's veins. Its head resembled a dragon, its horns a deer, its eyes a lion, its hooves an ox, and its tail a snake. Its body was covered in earth-yellow scales, radiating a reassuring aura of peace and a profound, all-encompassing Daoist essence. It was the Qilin!

This innate Qilin possessed an aura as steady as a mountain, and had also reached the Qi Refining stage. Its birth seemed to balance the slightly unbalanced laws of heaven and earth caused by the emergence of the dragon and phoenix, bringing stability to the "Law of Earth" and the concepts of "auspiciousness" and "benevolence."

The Qilin's feedback power surged in, allowing Chen Du's consciousness to gain a deeper understanding of "earth," "bearing capacity," and "life force." The insights from three different attributes and tendencies of the laws converged and collided in his consciousness. Although far from merging, they broadened his vision of the "Dao."

"Dragon, phoenix, Qilin… the ancestors of the three great primordial races were born on the same day?" Chen Du felt quite marvelous. Was this a coincidence, or an inevitable consequence of the world's origin being influenced by some will (perhaps his subconscious)?

With the birth of the three primordial deities, the primordial world seemed to have received a powerful boost. The world's barriers became visibly stronger (in Chen Du's perception), the internal space showed a subtle tendency to expand, and the laws of heaven and earth became increasingly clear and active. The world's energy level seemed to be advancing from a "micro-world" to a more stable level.

More primordial spirits were encouraged, accelerating their evolutionary process. Although far inferior to the three progenitors, they also began to materialize and awaken consciousness, presenting a vibrant and thriving scene throughout the primordial world.

Chen Du was immersed in the pleasure brought by the continuous feedback from the world, while meticulously observing the actions of these first batch of "children."

After becoming familiar with its power, the Ancestral Dragon began to patrol its ocean territory, instinctively driving the waters, enlightening the spirits of some weak aquatic creatures, and initially establishing its authority. The Phoenix perched atop the highest volcano in the west, preening its feathers, its flames moving at its will, and the phantoms of hundreds of birds (not yet born, but possessing the spiritual light of avian species) paid homage. The Qilin strolled across the land surrounding Mount Buzhou, where its presence brought lush vegetation, rising earth energy, and harmony to all things.

Everything seemed to be progressing in a positive direction.

However, Chen Du remained vigilant. He maintained a wisp of his divine sense, focusing on the direction from which the causal imprint had originated.

At the very moment the three primordial deities were born, and the primordial world's fortune surged and its laws manifested, in the depths of that distant dimension, the previously vaguely probing intent suddenly became clear and… intensely burning!

Like a drowsy hunter suddenly catching the intense scent of the most succulent prey!

A will far more powerful and condensed than the previous Devouring Spirit, like an invisible tentacle, pierced through layers of dimensional barriers, its undisguised greed and possessiveness locking onto the location of the Primordial World once more!

This time, it wasn't a questioning search, but a definite discovery, and a determined malice!

Chen Du's consciousness instantly turned cold.

"Finally...something decent has arrived."

He wasn't alarmed, but rather delighted. A powerful Devourer, the fragments of laws and primal power it contained, would undoubtedly far surpass that broken minion. For the growing Primordial World, it was both a crisis and...a great tonic!

Chen Du's will quietly spread, like the most patient spider, beginning to weave an invisible web across this dimensional region. He mobilized the primal power of the dimensional gaps, yet held back, letting a sliver of the Primordial World's aura leak out, like the most tempting bait.

"Come on, closer..." Chen Du murmured to himself, his eyes flashing with the cold light of a predator.

Within the primordial world, the Ancestral Dragon, the Primordial Phoenix, and the Primordial Qilin were completely unaware of this. They were still freely unleashing their nascent power, exploring this vast world that belonged to them. In the shadows of the world, however, an even greater storm was brewing.

The hunt across dimensional gaps and the evolution of the primordial world were about to intertwine once more. And this time, the commotion was destined to be far greater.



Chapter 5 Clash of Laws and Advancement of Boundaries


Chen Du's meticulously woven invisible net, suspended silently in the dimensional gap, released the faint, ethereal aura of the tiny, primordial world like bait. The powerful will from the distant depths was indeed drawn to this increasingly clear "fragrance of the world," its greed and urgency almost materializing, piercing through layers of nothingness and rushing towards him with overwhelming force.

This time, it was no small matter. Although the attacker's true form hadn't arrived, the core of its projected will was a hundred times more complete and condensed than the previous Devouring Spirit. It called itself the "Devouring Demon Lord," a malevolent entity born from the ruins of a shattered middle world, feeding solely on the essence of small and micro-worlds, a ferocious being even in the desolate regions of the dimensional gaps.
